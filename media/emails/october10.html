University of Chicago Runs Succesful Splash, Providing Free, Effective Programs to Over 400 Students

Students talk with a teacher at Chicago Splash! 2010.

For four years, the University of Chicago has been running a free Splash program to reach out to students from all backgrounds across Chicago and truly help everyone find what they love to learn.

Thanks to your support, Learning Unlimited has been able to provide the Chicago student group with free website software and day-of help to make the program a reality.

In addition to recruiting volunteer teachers, providing teacher training and support, and making hundreds of students feel at home on a college campus, the University of Chicago Splash Board devotes much of their efforts to outreach in the greater city of Chicago, sending Splash volunteers to talk about the programs at high schools, street fairs, and churches.

The outreach is working: this year's University of Chicago Splash was the University's largest Splash yet, and over 150 students who attended Splash also signed up to attend Cascade, the weekly afterschool program run by the same volunteers who run Splash.

Chicago-area students will have further opportunities for enrichment at Northwestern University in nearby Evanston this spring.

Students who attended Splash gave glowing feedback.  One student, Natalie, wrote on a survey, "Splash has showed me to go into depth with things you learn, not just taking notes, and saying 'okay, I'm done' but finding examples and looking deeper into new things you learn."  Another student, Lauren, wrote: "Splash has made me realize how many things I'm interested in besides the sciences."
