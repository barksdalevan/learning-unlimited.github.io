National Conference for Science and Technology in Out-of-School Time

Here's an interesting factoid. Most scientists first became interested in science before middle school, but they found their particular field in high school. That makes programs like Splash, Cascade, and HSSP perfect for budding scientists: they can explore a number of different areas and find what most interests them.

It's just one of the interesting things we learned last week while attending the National Conference for Science and Technology in Out-of-School Time. The conference brought together the vibrant community of providers of out-of-school activities for students of all ages with over 200 participants. Dan Zaharopol, Learning Unlimited's CEO, attended the conference to participate in discussions ranging across policy, research, and practice and to publicize LU's work.

You can find out more about the conference at the live blog.

President's Council Reports on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) Education

Making a big splash in the education world was the President's Council of Advisors on Science and Technology which released their plan for improving STEM education.

The very first recommendation of the report was that we must focus on both "preparation and inspiration." The report also calls for the creation of "opportunities for inspiration" outside the classroom, a call that we are happy to answer with Learning Unlimited programs across the country.
