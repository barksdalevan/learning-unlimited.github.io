UChicago and Northwestern Splash expand horizons for college-bound teen

Gaby Santoyo

Gaby Santoyo still keeps the origami she made two years ago at Northwestern Splash in a memory box in her bedroom.

Origami’s not the only thing Gaby was introduced to through Splash programs at Northwestern and the University of Chicago-- she has rehearsed hip-hop moves in front of a full-length mirror, ate Indian food, and listened to “indie” music, all for the very first time.

“Splash is a great way to experience college through your personal interests rather than through academic pressure,” she said.

Gaby, who lives in a neighborhood where fewer than 5% of adults have a bachelors’ degree, outperformed 93% of college-bound seniors on the ACT entrance exam and looks forward to applying to competitive colleges this fall.

Learning to think: A Former MIT Splash teacher shows high school students how

When he was a student at MIT, Paul Christiano taught classes through MIT Splash, MIT HSSP, and MIT Junction on topics ranging from auctions to theory in computer programming.

“There are a lot of cool topics that most student don’t see in high school, so by going further than the traditional curriculum, [MIT Splash and similar programs] are able to provided added value,’ Paul said. “I want to help make the world a better place.”

This summer, Paul ran SPARC at UC Berkeley, funded by Center for Applied Rationality. The program aims to show students how mathematics can lead to improved decision-making in everyday life. In the process of running SPARC, Paul consulted Learning Unlimited Daniel Zaharopol for assistance.
