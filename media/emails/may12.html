Duke Splash Teacher Honored for Science Research

Kenneth Hoehn teaches about fruit flies.

Kenneth Hoehn was one of 300 undergraduates to be awarded the Barry M. Goldwater Scholarship. The Scholarship was established by the U.S. Congress for students pursuing science-related careers.

"Growing up in the bible belt, evolutionary biology was the forbidden fruit," says Hoehn, who taught "Giant Sperm, Sterile Workers, and Other Oddities of Evolution" at Duke Splash this spring, a class inspired by the topics he thought about as a high school student and continues to talk about with college friends today.

In his Duke Splash class, Hoehn discussed Drosophilia bifurca, a fruit fly that produces sperm 20 times its length, and brought in a string 120 feet long to provide a sense of human scale. "After we passed it around the classroom we still had 30 yards to go," he said.

For Hoehn, Duke Splash allowed him to supplement his ongoing laboratory work in evolutionary biology with the teaching skills he hopes to use in graduate school and afterwards.
