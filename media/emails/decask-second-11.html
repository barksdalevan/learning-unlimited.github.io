Chris Kennedy: Why I Volunteer

Director of New Chapter Mentoring Chris Kennedy makes it possible for student leaders to run new Splashes, like this one at the University of Maryland, Baltimore County.

I remember teaching my first Splash class at MIT--a 3-hour advanced math class for about 20 intrepid high schoolers.

Now, 5 years later, I help recruit college student leaders to start their own Splash programs. This fall, we helped three colleges run their first Splash, and we're looking forward to another bushel in the spring. Many leaders of new Splash programs were once Splash students, some heard a friend raving about a Splash program, others just want to teach avant-garde poetry to high school students.

To continue expanding, we need the resources to reach out to promising college student leaders and ensure their success. Almost all of that work is done by volunteers like me, so your donation would go directly into the creation of a new Splash in St. Louis or the first wave of student recruitment in Atlanta.

This holiday season, please support Splash with a donation.


Even now, as LU enters its third year, we're only just getting started. With your help, we can continue creating new opportunities for tens of thousands of students across the country. Thank you for all your support!

Chris Kennedy
Director of New Chapter Mentoring
