Stanford Splash Student Wins $20,000 Scholarship

Kathy Pham at her high school graduation.

Kathy Pham of San Jose, California was one of 104 high school students to be honored with a $20,000 scholarship from the Horatio Alger Association. Scholarships are awarded on the basis of community leadership, perseverance, and financial need.

Kathy, who recently graduated from a high school where less than half of the graduating class attends a four-year college and about 70% of students' families qualify for AFDC, plans to study Russian through the State Department before heading to college in fall 2012.

Kathy first heard about Stanford Splash from a high school teacher and has participated in six Splash programs since 2008, taking courses ranging from "Explosive Chemistry" to "Cults: What’s the Deal?" She has encouraged other students from her high school to attend Splash, even talking to her principal about arranging a bus, which Stanford Splash would be able to fund and waive student registration fees.

"At Splash, I was able to take classes from college students and learn things I couldn't learn in high school, alongside high school students from all over the Bay Area," Kathy said.
