Dear Friends,

When I taught my first class at MIT Splash, one of my students told me that I made him want to be a astrophysicist. My perspective on the world changed when Ian told me that. I was 18 years old.

In the past year, Learning Unlimited helped over 6000 middle and high school students discover that they might want to study physics, or art history, or law, or video game music. Over 1000 college students volunteered to change lives.

We need your help to keep this network going. Although we're volunteer-driven, we need your contribution to coordinate 15 sites nation-wide, to provide software for student registration, and to help our mentors travel to advise college student leaders.

This is our final appeal of the year. Please help us maintain our work in 2012 by making a tax-deductible donation by December 31. Even $25 makes a big difference.

With your help, students everywhere can find what they love to learn. Thank you!
