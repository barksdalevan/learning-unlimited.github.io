Stanford Splash and MIT Splash Set New Student Attendance Records

Over 1,100 students attended Stanford Splash and over 2,400 students attended MIT Splash earlier in November. We saw many returning families and many first-timer families, suggesting that word-of-mouth alone contributes greatly to the success of our programs, so thank you for telling friends and family about the programs we help support!

Both Splash programs offered hundreds of choices to students eager to try out new topics and find what they love to learn. (MIT's program alone offered over 900 class sections!) Learning Unlimited ran Parents' Programs at both events, and many parents volunteered to help with our work. Meanwhile, representatives from young Splash programs at Boston College, Duke University, Boston University, the University of Maryland, Baltimore County, and UCLA all met to learn from MIT Splash's organizational model.

Boston College Runs First Splash

On November 6, Boston College students free Splash program that hosted over 170 students. Classes offered ranged from "Thinking about Thinking: Philosophy and Reasoning in the Western World" to "Lady Gaga in Pop Culture." Students got a more personalized experience with smaller classes and lots of freedom to take the classes that most interested them: over 100 courses were on offer.

Boston College students loved sharing their passions and leading the event. Plans are already underway for a Spring Splash.
