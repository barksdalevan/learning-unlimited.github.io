Long-Distance Splash Class is a First For LU

MIT student Michele Pratusevich and University of Maryland-Baltimore County student Philip Chang collaborated to teach "OPERATION: SPACE RANGERS" during the same time block on Saturday, March 10. One taught at UMBC Splash, and the other at MIT Spark. Students in both classes had to problem-solve in order to communicate with their long-distance peers over Skype.

"If I hadn’t hosted UMBC leaders earlier this year, I would have never have thought about doing a class like this," Michele said. "Not only did our students get to meet each other, but I have learned from UMBC Splash leaders."

Google Selects LU for Summer of Code

LU was one of 180 organizations invited by Google to participate in Summer of Code 2012, in which Google pays student software developers to contribute to open-access software projects. This will be LU’s second consecutive year in the program.

Google's Summer of Code intern will join LU's team of volunteers who work more than 1,000 hours per year building the website and software that helps college students run Splash programs.
