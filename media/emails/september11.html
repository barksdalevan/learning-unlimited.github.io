University of Chicago Remembers Splash! Chicago Leader Mandeep Bedi

Bedi, a recent graduate of the University of Chicago and a leader of Splash! Chicago, died in a traffic accident on August 25. The University of Chicago commemorates his life and his commitment to Splash! Chicago.

"Mandeep spent a tremendous amount of time volunteering for Splash! Chicago, and Splash Chicago relied on him for everything from printer paper to driving to pick up food. He volunteered to do the least glorious tasks because he believed in our program so much, and taught classes on topics close to his heart," said Amy Estersohn, a volunteer who worked with Bedi on Splash! Chicago.

"I believe we can solve the mysteries of the universe and save human lives at the same time"—MIT Splash Alum Michelle Borkin Delivers Lecture at TEDxBoston

Borkin's current research at Harvard University unites medical doctors, computer programmers, artists, and astronomers who are looking towards computer simulations to help solve problems. Ten years ago, her MIT Splash schedule looked surprisingly similar, with classes in calculus, computer programming, theoretical math, and astronomy.

"MIT Splash pushed me in the direction of pursuing science," Borkin said. "Though Splash was only a few days, it made that much of an impression on me.
