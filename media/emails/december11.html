LU December: How Topology Can Save the World, Spring Programs

Math was not Shaiesha Moore's favorite subject in high school. But in the winter of 2010, Moore signed up for a free Tuesday evening course in theoretical mathematics.

Moore learned about matrices, topology, and game theory from a University of Chicago student volunteer through Cascade, a program run by Splash at the University of Chicago and supported by Learning Unlimited.

Moore, who is now an international development major at Georgetown University's School of Foreign Service, finds her economics courses echo what she learned in Cascade. As one of the 1,000 Millennium Scholars chosen each year, her undergraduate and graduate education will be funded by the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation.

“I was really excited to be a part of Cascade. I felt like I was one of the college students even though I was just in high school. Just being on campus made me feel older,” she said.
