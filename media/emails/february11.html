News Briefs

This month, just three short items:

  • Duke ran their second Splash on February 19 to great success. Find out more on our blog.
  • Granite Hills High School, near San Diego, is running a Splash for middle school students this Saturday! Sign up now for the best classes.

What We're Reading

KQED predicts what curriculum will look like in the future. They say that Digital Delivery, Interest-Driven Learning, and "Skills 2.0" will drive curriculum. Explore their whole site for interesting articles.

Lawrence Mishel in The American Prospect argues against the common wisdom that current economic troubles are due to undereducation.

This NPR blog post discusses the state of science teaching in the country.

Finally, two articles from The New York Times. The first is a report that college students are more stressed than ever. The second is a column by Stanley Fish asking what Race to the Top is meant to get to the top of.
